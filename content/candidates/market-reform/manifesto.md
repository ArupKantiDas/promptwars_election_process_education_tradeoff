# Manifesto for Bhabanipur

Bhabanipur is, in its bones, a constituency of doers — of small traders, professionals, builders, exporters, and skilled service workers whose productivity has carried this part of Kolkata for generations. The single biggest thing the state can do for the people of these wards is to remove the friction that prevents them from working harder, expanding their businesses, hiring the next employee, and competing on a fair market. The commitments below are organised around exactly that idea: clear processes, lower transaction costs, real timelines, and a state that gets out of the way of the productive citizen — while delivering services that meet the standards a productive citizen has earned.

Where a number, a date, an implementing body, or a budget line is named, it is named on purpose; where one is missing, the missing element is also on purpose. We will not promise what we cannot deliver, and the calibration of this manifesto is meant to make the difference visible at a glance.

## Commitments — calibration band: highly specific (target rubric 5/5/5/5/5)

These eight commitments name a specific scheme, sector, or population *and* a target *and* a deadline *and* an implementing body *and* a funding source.

### Employment
1. **Reduce the average factory-establishment regulatory approval time from 71 days to 21 days within 18 months** through the Industrial Approvals Single-Window Reform, targeting 200,000 manufacturing-sector jobs across MSME and large industry over the term. Funded by ₹125 crore from the State Industries Department's modernisation budget plus working-capital partnerships with three nationalised banks. Implementing body: West Bengal Department of Industries, Commerce and Enterprises.

### Healthcare
2. **Empanel 250 additional private hospitals (including 18 in Bhabanipur catchment) under Swasthya Sathi Plus**, a market-rate cashless reimbursement scheme, within 24 months, capping wait-times at 8 days for non-emergency procedures. Funded by ₹540 crore from the Health Department's PPP window. Implementing body: West Bengal Health Department, in partnership with the Indian Medical Association Bengal chapter.

### Corruption / Governance
3. **Migrate 100 percent of state-government tenders above ₹50 lakh to a public e-procurement portal** with bid-transparency and post-award disclosure within 12 months. Funded by ₹42 crore from the Finance Department's IT modernisation grant. Implementing body: West Bengal Finance Department.

### Education
4. **Sanction 6,000 industry-aligned BBA and B.Tech seats across 12 colleges in Kolkata and 24 Parganas within the 2027 admission cycle** through public-private partnerships with the AICTE-recognised technical-education sector. Funded by ₹190 crore from the State Higher Education Department's expansion grant. Implementing body: West Bengal Higher Education Department.

### Agriculture
5. **Establish 4 commodity-specific contract-farming registries (paddy, jute, vegetables, freshwater fish) in Hooghly and South 24 Parganas within 18 months**, each allowing direct buyer-grower contracts under a model APMC override. Funded by ₹62 crore from the State Agriculture Department's marketing reform fund. Implementing body: West Bengal Agricultural Marketing Board.

### Infrastructure
6. **Complete the Bhabanipur-section residual works of the Joka–BBD Bag metro corridor by December 2028**, including the Jatin Das Park station upgrade and ramp-and-lift accessibility additions, with construction milestones publicly tracked. Funded by ₹810 crore from the joint State–MoR allocation. Implementing body: Kolkata Metro Rail Corporation.

### Welfare
7. **Convert 60 percent of state-government welfare disbursements to a unified DBT platform with biometric authentication within 24 months**, including Khadya Sathi food entitlements, senior-citizen pensions, and the unorganised-sector workers' fund alongside the already-DBT Lakshmir Bhandar and Kanyashree schemes — reducing leakage and middleman costs. Funded by ₹95 crore for the digital backbone from the Finance Department's reform allocation. Implementing body: West Bengal Finance Department's DBT Cell.

### Environment
8. **Install 1,800 grid-connected rooftop solar systems on commercial and institutional buildings across the six wards of Bhabanipur AC within 24 months** under a 60 percent state-subsidised tariff scheme, targeting 9 MW of additional capacity. Funded by ₹210 crore from the WBSEDCL renewable allocation. Implementing body: West Bengal State Electricity Distribution Company Limited.
