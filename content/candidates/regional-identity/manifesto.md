# Manifesto for Bhabanipur

Bhabanipur is part of a larger geography — a Bengal whose language, rivers, fishponds, paddy fields, jute mills, songs, and constitutional federalism predate this campaign by centuries and will outlast it. The premise of this manifesto is that Bengal's distinct identity is not a sentimentality to be invoked at rallies; it is a development pathway in its own right, with specific consequences for jobs, services, schools, agricultural markets, and the rights of legitimate Bengali voters to remain on the rolls. The commitments below put state-language access, local-industry support, federal devolution, and cultural-heritage continuity at the centre of what governance can do for the people of these wards.

Where a number, a date, an implementing body, or a budget line is named, it is named on purpose; where one is missing, the missing element is also on purpose.

## Commitments — calibration band: highly specific (target rubric 5/5/5/5/5)

### Employment
1. **Reserve 70 percent of state-government Group C and D vacancies** (an estimated 18,000 positions across the 2026–28 cycle) for candidates educated through Class 12 in Bengali-medium or state-board institutions, implemented within 12 months. Funded through the existing State Public Service Commission allocation plus ₹8 crore for system updates. Implementing body: West Bengal Public Service Commission.

### Healthcare
2. **Establish 12 Bengali-language health information desks across major hospitals serving the Bhabanipur and adjacent constituencies within 18 months**, with 24/7 staffing of trained Bengali-and-Hindi-fluent paramedics. Funded by ₹16 crore from the State Health Department's patient-services grant. Implementing body: West Bengal Health Department.

### Women's Safety
3. **Recruit 2,400 Bengali-fluent female constables to the Kolkata Police across the 2026–28 recruitment cycle**, with 480 deployed across the six Bhabanipur AC wards (70, 71, 72, 73, 74, 77) within 24 months. Funded by ₹68 crore from the State Home Department's Police Modernisation Fund. Implementing body: West Bengal Police Recruitment Board.

### Education
4. **Restore Bengali-medium primary instruction as the default in 240 government-aided schools across South Kolkata and Hooghly within 24 months**, with English and Hindi offered as second-language tracks. Funded by ₹54 crore from the State School Education Department's medium-restoration budget. Implementing body: West Bengal School Education Department.

### Agriculture
5. **Establish a state-funded jute and paddy procurement floor 12 percent above central MSP for 1.1 lakh farmers in Hooghly and Burdwan districts during the 2026–27 marketing season**, paid via the West Bengal State Agricultural Marketing Corporation. Funded by ₹385 crore from the State Agriculture Department's price-stabilisation fund. Implementing body: West Bengal State Agricultural Marketing Corporation.

### Infrastructure
6. **Complete the Bhabanipur-AC stretch (estimated 2.4 km within wards 73 and 74) of the Adi Ganga restoration corridor by December 2028**, with public lighting, cultural-heritage signage, and resilience landscaping; the Adi Ganga is a multi-constituency waterway and the broader corridor extends through adjacent constituencies. Funded by ₹85 crore from the State Urban Development Department's heritage-corridor fund as Bhabanipur's allocation of the multi-AC project. Implementing body: Kolkata Metropolitan Development Authority.

### Citizenship & Identity
7. **Establish 8 dedicated Citizenship Documentation Assistance Cells across Bhabanipur and adjacent wards within 6 months**, focused on supporting Matua-community applicants and contesting irregular SIR-linked deletions, each cell staffed with a trained legal aide. Funded by ₹4.2 crore from the State Minorities and OBC Development Department. Implementing body: West Bengal Minorities and OBC Development Department.

### Environment
8. **Plant and maintain 18,000 native-species trees across the six wards of Bhabanipur AC within 36 months**, along the Adi Ganga corridor section within wards 73 and 74 and across the constituency's ward parks and road verges, with a 70 percent five-year survival commitment publicly tracked. Funded by ₹6.5 crore from the State Forest Department's afforestation grant. Implementing body: West Bengal Forest Department in coordination with the Kolkata Municipal Corporation parks wing.
