# Manifesto for Bhabanipur

For the residents of every ward in Bhabanipur — from the trader on Harish Mukherjee Road to the home-based garment-stitcher in Padmapukur, from the senior citizen waiting for a hospital bed to the young graduate filling forms for an SSC vacancy that never opens — this is a manifesto written for a constituency that has too often been asked to wait. It is built on a single conviction: the test of governance is whether the most vulnerable household in our wards is materially better off five years from now than it is today.

The commitments below are organised by the priority issues we have heard repeatedly through the eighteen months of pre-campaign listening. Where a number, a date, an implementing body, or a budget line is named, it is named on purpose; where one is missing, the missing element is also on purpose. Every commitment is open to public scrutiny. Where we fall short, we expect to be told.

## Commitments — calibration band: highly specific (target rubric 5/5/5/5/5)

These eight commitments name a specific scheme, sector, or population *and* a target *and* a deadline *and* an implementing body *and* a funding source. They are the commitments we expect to be measured against publicly.

### Employment
1. **Recruit 12,000 paramedical and ASHA-supervisor positions** across the 87 wards of South Kolkata (including Bhabanipur) within 24 months, prioritising women graduates aged 22–35 from BPL households. Funded by ₹185 crore from the State Health Department's 2026–27 budget. Implementing body: West Bengal Health & Family Welfare Department.

### Healthcare
2. **Expand Swasthya Sathi cover** from ₹5 lakh to ₹8 lakh per family per year for all Bhabanipur-resident families by April 2027, adding 14 specialty conditions (including paediatric oncology and high-risk obstetrics) to the cashless scheme. Funded by ₹620 crore from the state Health Department's expansion grant. Implementing body: West Bengal Swasthya Sathi Samiti.

### Women's Safety
3. **Establish 3 new Mahila Suraksha One-Stop Centres** covering the six wards of Bhabanipur AC (70, 71, 72, 73, 74, 77), one centre serving each pair of wards, within 18 months; each staffed with 2 female sub-inspectors, a counsellor, and a panel lawyer; centres open 24/7. Funded by ₹14 crore from the State Home Department's Nirbhaya Fund tranche. Implementing body: West Bengal Police Women Safety Wing.

### Corruption / Governance
4. **Publish quarterly RTI-style disclosure dashboards** covering all KMC ward-level expenditure above ₹2 lakh, beginning with the FY 2026–27 Q2 report, with a 14-day grievance redressal SLA. Funded through the existing KMC IT budget plus ₹3.2 crore for dashboard build. Implementing body: Kolkata Municipal Corporation IT and Accounts wings, audited by the State Vigilance Commission.

### Education
5. **Hire 4,500 permanent primary-school teachers** across South 24 Parganas and Kolkata districts (covering Bhabanipur catchment) within 18 months, through a transparent SSC examination cleared of past procedural irregularities. Funded by ₹312 crore from the State School Education Department's recruitment fund. Implementing body: West Bengal School Service Commission.

### Agriculture
6. **Top-up ₹2,500 per acre per season for paddy and ₹1,800 per acre for jute** as a state-funded supplement to MSP, for 1.4 lakh small and marginal farmers across Hooghly and South 24 Parganas in the 2026–27 kharif and rabi seasons. Funded by ₹460 crore from the State Agriculture Department's price-support window. Implementing body: West Bengal Agricultural Marketing Board.

### Welfare
7. **Raise Lakshmir Bhandar monthly transfer** from ₹1,000 to ₹1,500 for general-category beneficiaries and from ₹1,200 to ₹1,800 for SC/ST beneficiaries, effective April 2027, covering all 1.21 crore enrolled women statewide (estimated 78,000 in Bhabanipur and adjacent wards). Funded by ₹4,800 crore additional allocation from the State Women & Child Development Department. Implementing body: West Bengal Women & Child Development Department.

### Citizenship & Identity
8. **Open 6 dedicated Voter-Roll Verification Camps** across the 6 wards of Bhabanipur (70, 71, 72, 73, 74, 77) within 90 days of taking office, with an ECI-trained Booth Level Officer attending each camp, focused on documenting and contesting any SIR-linked deletion of legitimate voters. Funded by ₹85 lakh from the State Election Commission's outreach budget. Implementing body: Office of the Chief Electoral Officer, West Bengal.
