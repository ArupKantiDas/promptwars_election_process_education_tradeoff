# Manifesto for Bhabanipur

The single biggest reform Bhabanipur needs is the boring kind: clear processes, visible decisions, public dashboards, ombudspeople with real powers, and the simple insistence that anyone collecting a public salary works for the public, not the other way around. Nothing in this manifesto is glamorous. Most of it is what governance is supposed to be when there is nothing to hide. The commitments below are organised around exactly that idea: traceability, transparency, citizen access, and accountability with consequences attached.

Where a number, a date, an implementing body, or a budget line is named, it is named on purpose; where one is missing, the missing element is also on purpose. We will not promise what we cannot deliver, and we ask the voter to read the calibration of this manifesto closely.

## Commitments — calibration band: highly specific (target rubric 5/5/5/5/5)

### Employment
1. **Publish all 18,000+ state-government job vacancies on a single citizen portal within 6 months**, with real-time application status, transparent merit-list disclosure, automatic SLA flags, and a public ombudsperson handling grievances inside 14 working days. Funded by ₹38 crore from the State Personnel Department's IT modernisation allocation. Implementing body: West Bengal Department of Personnel and Administrative Reforms.

### Healthcare
2. **Mandate publication of pricing, wait-time, and service-level data for all 92 state-government hospitals (including 14 in South Kolkata) on a citizen-facing dashboard within 12 months**, with monthly third-party verification. Funded by ₹22 crore from the State Health Department's transparency budget. Implementing body: West Bengal Health Department.

### Women's Safety
3. **Establish 4 state-funded fast-track courts at Kolkata judicial-district venues for crimes against women**, with priority docket allocation for cases originating in Bhabanipur AC and the surrounding South Kolkata sub-divisions, targeting case disposal within 9 months from FIR, beginning April 2027. Funded by ₹95 crore from the State Judicial Department's fast-track allocation. Implementing body: West Bengal Department of Judicial Affairs.

### Corruption / Governance
4. **Pass a Bhabanipur-Constituency Asset Disclosure and Lokayukta Strengthening Bill within the first legislative session**, requiring all elected representatives in the constituency to publish quarterly asset disclosures and submit to a Lokayukta with prosecutorial referral powers. Funded by ₹4.8 crore from the State Vigilance Commission's expansion grant. Implementing body: West Bengal State Vigilance Commission.

### Education
5. **Make all SSC, TET, and higher-education recruitment merit lists, paper evaluations, and answer-key challenges publicly accessible on a single portal within 12 months**, including digital records of all written-test answer scripts on request. Funded by ₹26 crore from the State Education Department's transparency allocation. Implementing body: West Bengal School Service Commission.

### Infrastructure
6. **Publish a real-time KMC public-works tracker covering all 6 Bhabanipur wards' (70, 71, 72, 73, 74, 77) road, drainage, and lighting projects above ₹10 lakh, with photographs and milestone updates, by July 2027.** Funded by ₹3.6 crore from the KMC IT budget. Implementing body: Kolkata Municipal Corporation.

### Welfare
7. **Conduct a 100 percent biometric verification of all welfare beneficiaries in Bhabanipur (estimated 1.4 lakh enrolments across schemes) within 18 months**, with a public exception-and-grievance portal protecting genuine beneficiaries from wrongful exclusion. Funded by ₹18 crore from the Finance Department's audit-reform allocation. Implementing body: West Bengal Finance Department's DBT Cell.

### Citizenship & Identity
8. **Set up a state-funded SIR Audit Cell within 90 days of taking office**, staffed with 12 trained legal aides and 3 ECI-experienced supervisors, conducting case-by-case review of every SIR-linked deletion across the 6 Bhabanipur wards (70, 71, 72, 73, 74, 77; estimated 18,000 affected entries). Funded by ₹6.4 crore from the State Election Commission's reform budget. Implementing body: Office of the Chief Electoral Officer, West Bengal.

## Closing

A reform manifesto is not a manifesto written by people who think governance is broken; it is a manifesto written by people who think governance can be measured, and that what gets measured improves. The eight specific commitments above are the measurable test. The eight aspirational ones at the bottom are honest about being aspirations — they will not, on their own, fix anything. If we deliver the specific eight, the aspirational eight start to look less like rhetoric. If we don't, voters should hold us to the rhetoric we earned.
